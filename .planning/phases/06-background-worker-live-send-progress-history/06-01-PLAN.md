<objective>
Build the campaign-level state-machine seams the worker drives: the atomic claim (the DB-as-queue win signal), the crash-recovery orphan sweep, and the terminal finalize transitions. These are the three single-statement `campaigns` / `send_records` UPDATE seams that make a live send crash-safe and resumable (SEND-01 / SEND-06).

Purpose: A background send must survive a worker crash. That guarantee is entirely in these three seams — claim serializes ownership, recover neutralizes in-flight rows so no recipient is double-sent, and finalize records the outcome. Everything else (materialize, send loop) composes over them.
Output: `lib/worker/{claim,recover,finalize}.ts` + their node:test seams, all green under `npm test`.
</objective>

## Phase Goal (user story)

**As a** signed-in sender, **I want to** have my queued campaign sent in a crash-safe background job with live progress and a durable per-recipient record, **so that** I can reliably deliver my whole batch, watch it happen, resume it after any crash without double-sending, and later review or download exactly what was sent.
